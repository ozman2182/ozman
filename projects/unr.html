University of Nevada, Reno Main Website

Overview

The University of Nevada, Reno website serves resources and information to the Reno/Sparks community, faculty & staff, parents, and over 21,000 current students. The website is on a content management system that helps department marketing specialists maintain their content and ensures accessibility of new web components.

Problem

In 2017 the university needed to redesign their website due to inaccessible content on more than 15,000 pages and allow marketing specialists to easily update their own department pages.

Role

My role was to research, design and test all new UIs for accessibility. I collaborated with the marketing web team and marketing specialists to launch this project.

Discovery and research

We wanted to better understand how our users find out about the university, what kind of information was most important to them, how easy it is for them to find that information, and what devices they access the website. Some of the research methods include:

  • Competitive research to compare how other regional universities organize their content and structure their navigation.
  • Schedule in-person interview questions to prospective students, parents, and faculty to determine their expectations and impressions of the current website.
  • Discover the top tasks our users want to achieve while visiting the website.
  • Use analytics to determine the most common devices our on-campus and off-campus users use when visiting the website.

Personas and Information architecture

After reviewing the research information, I created personas of our users and identified the most important information our users search for on the website. We reviewed this information and collaborated on a new architecture of the website to help our users find information on the website.

Wireframe and low-fidelity prototypes

Next, I worked on basic wireframes and prototypes to present new website feature ideas. After iterating through design options, I conducted usability studies with our users to see how they interacted with the prototype. Some of the goals I wanted to achieve include:

  • Introduce a modular web component approach to allow web specialists to build pages for their departments.
  • Explore ideas for global navigation and local section navigation.
  • Identify how users find information using the prototype.

Visual design and prototype

After deciding on a navigation structure and design direction, I started working on the visual designs of the new website using Sketch and Invision. After finalizing the designs I helped present the new designs to the administrative faculty.

Challenges

Getting buy-in during the research phase was difficult at the beginning of the project because many of my team members wanted to see wireframes or something more visual. After moving into the wireframe and prototype phase it helped to reference back to the research and justify some of the design decisions I presented.

Accessibility

This project helped grow my knowledge in accessibility by collaborating closely with our campus accessibility manager throughout the design process. As I worked through my design ideas, I was able to articulate them to our team and show how someone using a screen reader would interact with the new website.

Learnings

  • I learned a lot more about front-end development. Being in a 4 person web team, we did not have a dedicated front-end developer to work on the UI. I helped develop many of the web components layouts on the website.
  • Many of the departments and units across the university felt that their section pages needed to be on the main university navigation. It was difficult to design a structure with navigation that could accommodate a lot of links. Using analytics we were able to communicate why we picked the most important links to highlight the university.
